Cursor FX

Cursor effects focus viewers' attention on cursor movement and mouse clicks. Cursor effects can only be applied to recording files (.trec or .cmrec).

Add a cursor effect:

  1. Click the Cursor FX tab.
  2. Click the Cursor Effect, Left Click Effect, or Right Click Effect tabs to view the available cursor effects.
    • Cursor Effects: Follow the cursor movement and enhance the cursor throughout the duration of the effect.
    • Left Click Effects: Display an effect around the cursor when the left mouse button is clicked.
    • Right Click Effects: Display an effect around the cursor when the right mouse button is clicked.
  1. Drag the desired cursor effect onto a recording file (.trec or .cmrec) on the timeline.

Customize cursor effects properties:

  1. Click to select the clip on the timeline with the cursor effects.
  2. Click the Properties button to open the properties.
  3. Click the Cursor Properties tab to adjust the cursor effects properties. See Properties.

Change the duration:

By default, cursor effects apply to the entire duration of a recording file (.trec or .cmrec) on the timeline.

  1. Click the arrow on the bottom of a clip on the timeline to show the applied effects.
  2. Drag the ends of the cursor effects to adjust the duration.

Remove a cursor effect:

  • From the timeline: Click the arrow on the bottom of a clip on the timeline to show the applied effects. Click to select the effect to delete. Press the Delete key.
  • From the properties: Click to select the clip on the timeline. On the Cursor Effects tab in the properties, click the Remove button for the desired effect.
