Keystroke effect

By default, Camtasia captures keystroke data. When your recording is saved as a .trec or .cmrec, Camtasia can automatically generate keystrokes using that data.

Add auto-generated keystrokes to your recording:

  1. Click the Video FX tab.
  2. Click and drag the Keystroke effect from the Video FX tab onto a recording (the recording must be added to the timeline first). A message on the timeline indicates that the effect has been added to your media clip.



    This effect can only be added to recordings that have keystroke data collected. To show keyboard activity for recordings that did not utilize keystrokes, use keystroke callouts.
  3. To reposition the auto-generated keystrokes, click on the red anchor point and drag to a new position on the canvas.
  4. To edit the effect, select the media and open the properties by clicking the Properties button.

Keystroke properties:

In the properties, you can change the style, duration, and size of the Keystroke effect.

  • To change the style, duration, and size, open the properties. Click the Basic Properties and Effects tab.
  • To reset to the style's default properties, click the Reset or Reset All button.
