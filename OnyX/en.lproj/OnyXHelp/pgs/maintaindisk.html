Structure of the disk

You can use OnyX to verify the structure of the system files on the startup disk and to provide the option of running this verification upon opening OnyX.

Warning: During the verification process, the Finder can become slow or unresponsive. The wait cursor (which looks like a colored pinwheel) can appear. This is normal, so wait for the verification to finish.

Important: This verification option is only possible if the hard disk is journaled. To turn on the journaling, go to the Disk pane of the Info toolbar item.

Open OnyX, and enter your administrator password.

Click the Maintenance toolbar button; then select the "Structure of the Disk" tab.

Click the Check button to immediately verify the startup disk.

By default, OnyX asks whether you want to check the structure of the startup disk when OnyX opens. You can choose to automatically check it or to do nothing by selecting the corresponding option in the Advanced pane of OnyX preferences.
