Editing ScreenFlow Projects : Using Nested Clips
Using Nested Clips
At some point in the editing process, you may find that your project—after adding more and more clips—is becoming unmanageable. The solution: create nested clips.
Figure 39. Screencast with Nested Clips
You can select multiple, related clips and merge them into a single, nested clip—this reduces the visual complexity of the high-level timeline by displaying a single nested clip—which may represent dozens of clips gathered inside it.
Each nested clip can be expanded and displayed on its own tab (Figure 39), so that you can edit it on its own independent view of the timeline. Nested clips also make it easy to re-use complex sequences in other places or other projects. But more importantly, you can edit a whole group of clips by placing edits on the nest, which affect all of the clips inside the nest.
